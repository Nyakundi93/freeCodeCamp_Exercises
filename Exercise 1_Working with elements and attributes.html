Masala Tea Recipe

Here is a simple recipe for your Masala Tea.

Ingredients

  • Tea Leaves
  • Clean Drinking Water
  • Masala (Spices)
    • 150g Ground ginger (tangawizi)
    • 50g Ground cardamom (iliki)
    • 50g Ground cinnamon (mdaalasin)
    • 50g Whole black peppercorns (pilipili manga)
    • 25g Whole cloves (karafuu)
    • Optional: 10g Fennel seeds, 1/2 nutmeg
  • Raw Boiled Milk
  • Sugar

Instructions

  1. Masala Preperation
  2. Tea Preperation

Masala Preparation Instructions

  1. Roast Spices: Lightly toast the cloves, peppercorns, cinnamon, and cardamom in a dry pan on low heat until aromatic (approx. 2-3 minutes).
  2. Grind: Once cooled, grind the toasted spices to a fine powder using a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
  3. Mix & Store: Mix the ground spices with the ground ginger. Store in an airtight container for up to a month.

How To Make the Tea

  1. Boil Spices: Bring 1-2 cups of water to a boil, adding 1 teaspoon of your homemade tea masala powder.
  2. Add Tea: Add 2 tablespoons of black tea leaves and boil for 2 minutes to create a strong decoction.
  3. Add Milk & Sweeten: Pour in 1-2 cups of full-fat milk and sugar to taste.
  4. Simmer: Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes for the flavors to meld, allowing the color to turn a deep caramel brown.

The tea is now ready. Strain into mugs and serve hot with choice accompaniment.

Key Tips:

  • Fresh Ginger: While you can use dry ginger powder, fresh crushed ginger is commonly used for a stronger kick.
  • Ratio: For the best, richest tea, many Kenyans use a ratio of 2 cups water to 1 cup milk.
  • Star Anise: Adding a single star anise adds a sweet, licorice flavor to the blend.
